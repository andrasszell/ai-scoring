# AI Adoption Intelligence Platform

## Technical Architecture Specification (V1)

# Objective

Build a probabilistic intelligence platform that estimates how deeply a company uses Artificial Intelligence.

The system should distinguish between:

* AI marketing
* AI experimentation
* AI adoption
* AI integration
* AI-native operations
* AI-first companies

The goal is not to count AI-related words.

The goal is to estimate the latent AI maturity state of a company using publicly available evidence.

---

# Core Question

For any company:

> How deeply is AI integrated into the organization?

This includes:

* products
* operations
* engineering
* hiring
* research
* decision making
* customer support
* internal workflows

---

# Core Principle

AI adoption is treated as a hidden variable.

We do not observe AI adoption directly.

We observe evidence generated by the company.

Examples:

* job postings
* websites
* technical blogs
* patents
* research papers
* GitHub repositories
* product documentation
* earnings calls
* press releases

The objective is to estimate:

P(AI_State_t | Evidence)

---

# AI Maturity Model

Each company is represented by a latent AI state vector.

```python
AI_State = [
    ai_awareness,
    ai_usage,
    ai_integration,
    ai_productization,
    ai_operations,
    ai_research
]
```

---

# Latent Dimensions

## 1. AI Awareness

Evidence that the company understands AI.

Examples:

* executive statements
* AI strategy documents
* AI roadmap discussions

Question:

"Does the company understand AI?"

---

## 2. AI Usage

Evidence of AI tool adoption.

Examples:

* Copilot
* ChatGPT Enterprise
* Claude
* Cursor
* AI coding assistants

Question:

"Are employees using AI tools?"

---

## 3. AI Integration

Evidence that AI is embedded into workflows.

Examples:

* customer support automation
* AI-assisted sales
* AI-powered analytics
* internal agents

Question:

"Does AI affect daily operations?"

---

## 4. AI Productization

Evidence that AI appears in products.

Examples:

* AI features
* recommendation systems
* AI APIs
* AI assistants

Question:

"Does the company sell AI-powered capabilities?"

---

## 5. AI Operations

Evidence that business processes depend on AI.

Examples:

* automated support
* document processing
* knowledge management
* forecasting systems

Question:

"Could the company operate without AI?"

---

## 6. AI Research

Evidence of proprietary AI capability.

Examples:

* AI patents
* AI publications
* AI engineers
* foundation model work

Question:

"Is the company creating AI rather than consuming it?"

---

# Final AI Score

The final score is derived from latent dimensions.

```python
AI_SCORE =
w1 * ai_awareness +
w2 * ai_usage +
w3 * ai_integration +
w4 * ai_productization +
w5 * ai_operations +
w6 * ai_research
```

The score is reported as:

```text
AI Score = 78 ± 5
```

# AI Evidence Extraction Layer

Each document is processed by an LLM.

Prompt:

```text
Estimate evidence for:

1. AI Awareness
2. AI Usage
3. AI Integration
4. AI Productization
5. AI Operations
6. AI Research

Return:
- score
- confidence
- explanation
```

Example output:

```json
{
  "ai_awareness": 0.9,
  "ai_usage": 0.6,
  "ai_integration": 0.4,
  "ai_productization": 0.2,
  "ai_operations": 0.3,
  "ai_research": 0.0,
  "confidence": 0.85
}
```

# Source Credibility Model

Not all evidence is equal.

Example:

| Source                  | Weight |
| ----------------------- | ------ |
| Patent                  | 1.0    |
| Research Paper          | 0.95   |
| Technical Documentation | 0.9    |
| GitHub                  | 0.9    |
| Earnings Call           | 0.85   |
| Job Posting             | 0.8    |
| Company Blog            | 0.6    |
| Marketing Page          | 0.4    |

---

# Novelty Detection

Avoid counting duplicate information.

Example:

100 news articles repeating one announcement should contribute approximately once.

Use:

```python
embedding_similarity
```

and clustering.

---

# Bayesian AI State Estimation

For each company:

```python
X_t =
[
 awareness,
 usage,
 integration,
 productization,
 operations,
 research
]
```

Observations update the posterior:

```python
P(X_t | Evidence)
```

using particle filtering.

# MVP Scope

Only build:

```text
Documents
    ↓
LLM Evidence Extraction
    ↓
Credibility Weighting
    ↓
Bayesian State Estimation
    ↓
AI Score
```

Do NOT build:

* knowledge graph
* forecasting
* advanced causal inference
* dynamic graph systems

until the AI score itself is validated.

---

# Success Metric

The system should correctly distinguish:

* AI marketing companies
* AI adopters
* AI-native companies

even when they use similar language publicly.

The goal is to measure actual AI penetration, not AI publicity.
